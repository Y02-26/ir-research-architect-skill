# Checklists

Use these for fast critique, grading, or revision.

## Research Design Checklist

- Topic is narrower than a field and broader than a single fact.
- Puzzle identifies an anomaly, contrast, change, or unresolved debate.
- Research question is clear, meaningful, and answerable.
- Dependent variable Y is explicit.
- Independent variable X is explicit.
- X plausibly precedes Y.
- Mechanism connects cause and outcome.
- Scope conditions are stated when needed.
- Alternative explanations are named.
- Concepts are defined before indicators are chosen.
- Indicators are observable.
- Evidence is accessible and appropriate.
- Method matches the question.
- Variable-control strategy is justified.
- Inference risks are acknowledged.

## Full Workflow Checklist

- Topic: concrete phenomenon, actors/cases, time range, and observable variation.
- Research question: asks why/how/under what conditions and includes an outcome.
- Literature review: organizes user-provided sources by explanation type or debate.
- Causal explanation: states X, Y, mechanism, counterfactual logic, and alternatives.
- Hypotheses: include direction, condition if needed, mechanism, and testable implications.
- Variables: distinguish independent, dependent, mediating, moderating, confounding, collider, and control variables.
- Operationalization: includes definitions, dimensions, indicators, sources, coding rules, validity, and reliability.
- Hypothesis testing: specifies evidence, method, case/sample logic, controls, alternatives, and disconfirming evidence.
- Thesis structure: each chapter serves the research question.

## Causal Explanation Checklist

- Y is defined before choosing a favorite explanation.
- Variation in Y is clear.
- X precedes Y and is not defined by Y.
- Mechanism identifies actors/entities, attributes, activities, sequence, and generative links.
- Each mechanism step has evidence implications.
- Confounders are considered and separated from mediators.
- Collider variables are drawn accurately: in the source Figure 5.5 pattern, X -> Y, X -> D, and Y -> D.
- Colliders are not controlled in a way that creates collider stratification bias.
- Counterfactual reasoning is historically plausible.
- Case comparison is causally meaningful.
- The claim does not exceed the evidence or the stated scope.

## Hypothesis Checklist

- Hypothesis contains X, Y, direction, and mechanism.
- Scope condition is included when the claim is bounded.
- It is testable and can fail.
- It is not circular or tautological.
- It does not mix several unrelated mechanisms.
- At least one alternative hypothesis is considered.
- Disconfirming evidence is named.

## Operationalization Checklist

- Conceptual definition is not circular.
- Dimensions are separated.
- Indicator matches the dimension.
- Data source is named.
- Coding rule is explicit.
- Validity risk is discussed.
- Reliability check is possible.
- Missing data and bias are acknowledged.

## Hypothesis Testing Checklist

- Main and alternative hypotheses are stated.
- Evidence is linked to observable implications.
- Case selection logic is explained.
- Control variables are theoretically justified.
- Mediators and colliders are not controlled incorrectly.
- Negative evidence is considered.
- Conclusion distinguishes support from proof.

## Thesis Writing Checklist

- Introduction presents puzzle, question, argument, method, and contribution.
- Literature review creates a debate map.
- Theory section yields hypotheses.
- Method section explains case/data choice.
- Empirical chapters test rather than merely narrate.
- Conclusion answers the question directly.
- Citations are consistent and complete.
