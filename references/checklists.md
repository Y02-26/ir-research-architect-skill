# Checklists

Use these for fast critique, grading, or revision.

## Research Design Checklist

- Topic is narrower than a field and broader than a single fact.
- Puzzle identifies an anomaly, contrast, change, or unresolved debate.
- Research question is clear, meaningful, and answerable.
- Dependent variable is explicit.
- Independent variable is explicit.
- Mechanism connects cause and outcome.
- Scope conditions are stated.
- Rival explanations are named.
- Concepts are defined before use.
- Indicators are observable.
- Evidence is accessible and appropriate.
- Method matches the question.
- Variable control strategy is justified.
- Inference risks are acknowledged.

## Full Workflow Checklist

- **选题**: names a concrete phenomenon, actors/cases, time range, and observable variation.
- **研究问题**: asks why/how/under what conditions; includes an explicit outcome.
- **文献综述设计**: groups user-provided sources by explanation type or debate; identifies a real gap.
- **因果解释**: states X, Y, mechanism, scope condition, and rival explanations.
- **假设**: has direction, condition, mechanism, and observable implications.
- **变量**: distinguishes independent, dependent, mediating, moderating, confounding, and control variables.
- **操作化**: defines concepts, dimensions, indicators, sources, coding rules, validity, and reliability.
- **假设检验**: specifies evidence, method, case/sample logic, controls, rivals, and falsifying evidence.
- **论文结构**: each chapter serves the research question and testing strategy.

## Literature Review Checklist

- Literature is grouped by debate, explanation, mechanism, or method.
- Each group is connected to the research question.
- Key assumptions and evidence are identified.
- The review states what existing literature cannot yet explain.
- The user's contribution follows logically from the gap.
- Recent and classic works are balanced when possible.

## Causal Explanation Checklist

- X precedes Y.
- Mechanism has identifiable steps.
- Each step has observable implications.
- Rival mechanisms are plausible and testable.
- Confounders are considered.
- Mediators are not accidentally controlled away.
- The causal graph is coherent.
- The claim does not exceed the evidence.

## Operationalization Checklist

- Conceptual definition is not circular.
- Dimensions are separated.
- Indicator matches the dimension.
- Data source is named.
- Coding rule is explicit.
- Validity risk is discussed.
- Reliability check is possible.
- Missing data and bias are acknowledged.

## Hypothesis Testing Checklist

- Main and rival hypotheses are stated.
- Test evidence is linked to observable implications.
- Case selection logic is explained.
- Control variables are theoretically justified.
- Negative evidence is considered.
- Uncertainty is reported.
- Conclusion distinguishes support from proof.

## Thesis Writing Checklist

- Introduction presents puzzle, question, argument, method, and contribution.
- Literature review creates a debate map.
- Theory section yields hypotheses.
- Method section explains case/data choice.
- Empirical chapters test rather than narrate.
- Conclusion answers the question directly.
- Citations are consistent and complete.
